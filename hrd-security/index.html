Clean Clothes Campaign

 

developing a practical protocol

for digital security

Aims

  • Reduce risk to partners
  • Increase awareness of safety aspects
  • Develop active threat and response model

Trade-off: comfort, practicality and security

  • how much effort am I prepared to spend to secure the communications ?
  • can I expect the same from the other people in the conversation ?
  • can my action (or lack of it) hurt other people in the conversation ?

This analysis would ideally be made before each conversation;

where that is not possible we should at least have a clear idea of the risks and trade-offs that you engage in by not having a secure conversation.

modes of conversation

  • synchronous (same time) or asynchronous
  • chat, files, voice, video
  • one-on-one, within a close-knit group, with a more open group

A further complication is that the threat model can be different per country/region, and that there is a distinction between security and privacy.

Email

This is difficult, because of ingrained behaviour, and the fact that email is a mixture of one-on-one and group conversation.

It would be better to replace group email communication with more modern forms (www.mattermost.org for self-hosted Slack alternative)

one-on-one (and small group) secure email should be GPG, but barrier to use is relatively high, although mailvelope works for important webmail providers (gmail, yahoo) and enigmail for imap.

But ultimately, the key to more email security is

less email

Any in-transit security obtained is obviously meaningless if the security of the partners and their devices is compromised.

Travel security (phones, laptops)

  • would it be bad if it got stolen by a thief?
  • would it be bad if it got stolen by government?
