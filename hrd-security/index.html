Trade-off: comfort, practicality and security

how much effort am I prepared to spend to secure the communications ?

can I expect the same from the other people in the conversation ?

can my action (or lack of it) HURT other people? How bad?

This analysis would ideally be made before each conversation;

where that is not possible we should at least have a clear idea of the risks and trade-offs that you engage in by not having a secure conversation.

modes of conversation

synchronous (same time) or asynchronous

chat, files, voice, video

one-on-one, within a close-knit group, with a more open group

A further complication is that the threat model can be different per country/region, and that there is a distinction between security and privacy.

Email

This is difficult, because of learned behaviour, and the fact that email is a mixture of one-on-one and group conversation.

It would be better to replace group email communication with more modern forms (Rocketchat or mattermost )

one-on-one (and small group) secure email should use GPG, but barrier to use is relatively high, although mailvelope works for important webmail providers (gmail, yahoo) and enigmail for imap.

But ultimately, the key to more email security is

less email
